Method Overview

Our method takes existing 3DGS assets as input and refines and aligns them without requiring additional training, enabling crowdsourced construction of modular indoor digital twins.

1. 3DGS Asset Refinement

Asset refinement converts each room-level 3DGS asset into a clean module by removing exterior and boundary-crossing Gaussians, then baking their appearance onto boundary mesh textures. This keeps the view from inside the room while preventing neighboring modules from visually colliding after alignment.

Why Is Refinement Needed?

Indoor 3DGS reconstruction often creates large, blurry Gaussians around room boundaries to represent low-frequency wall color and lighting. A single asset can still look plausible, but when multiple room modules are connected, these Gaussians can leak into adjacent spaces and cause visible overlap. Refinement removes those out-of-room contributions and replaces boundary appearance with mesh textures.

Why Remove Boundary-Crossing Gaussians?

Removing only Gaussians whose centers are outside the room is not enough. Some Gaussians remain centered inside the room but still extend across the boundary plane. When those Gaussians overlap with the boundary mesh, line-shaped intersection artifacts appear. We therefore remove Gaussians whose effective rendering range crosses the boundary and bake their appearance onto the mesh instead.

2. Door Extraction

Door extraction segments the door region with SAM3 and computes intersections with the wall boundary plane to estimate reliable 3D door corners for alignment.

3. Door-Based Alignment

Corresponding door corners define the transformation matrix between modules. The matrix is then used to connect a new room asset to the existing digital twin.

4. Door Interaction

Door meshes and Gaussians are separated into independent door assets. Gaussians crossing the door-wall boundary are split so the door side moves with the door while the wall side remains in the static room asset.

PSNR measures pixel-level similarity; higher is better (>30 dB: high, 25-30 dB: moderate, <25 dB: noticeable difference).
