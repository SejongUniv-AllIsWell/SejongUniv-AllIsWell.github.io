Modutwin builds indoor digital twins by refining room-level 3D Gaussian Splatting assets, detecting doors, and registering independent modules to a shared basemap.

Abstract

Modutwin is a web platform for constructing building-scale indoor digital twins from independently captured 3D Gaussian Splatting assets. The system refines room-level assets by removing outer Gaussians and generating boundary meshes, extracts door geometry with SAM3-assisted corner detection, and aligns modules to a fixed basemap through door-based geometric registration. This workflow enables modular indoor reconstruction while preserving interactive door assets in the final scene.

Pipeline

1

Refine 3DGS Assets

Remove boundary-crossing Gaussians and synthesize clean boundary surfaces.

2

Extract Doors

Detect door corners using SAM3 or user-provided four-point annotations.

3

Align Modules

Register modules to the basemap with a door-to-door similarity transform.

4

Interact

Keep door geometry grouped so the final scene supports open/close motion.

3DGS Asset Refinement

Indoor assets often contain geometry outside the intended room boundary. Refinement removes overlapping Gaussians and reconstructs boundary meshes for modular composition.

Door Extraction and Alignment

Interactive Door Assets

Door mesh and splat assets remain grouped after alignment, enabling open/close interaction in the composed indoor scene instead of treating doors as static wall texture.
